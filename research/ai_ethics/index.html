Fairness in Clinical Large Language Models

The integration of large language models in clinical applications underscores the significance of fairness. Given their potential to shape medical decisions, diagnostics, and patient outcomes, these models necessitate rigorous examination. The conjunction of AI with healthcare introduces a distinct set of ethical challenges, underscoring the primacy of fairness. Central to this discourse is the question:

Do clinical large language models provide equitable outcomes across diverse populations?

Achieving fairness in clinical large language models extends beyond technology to ethical considerations. Models, when inadequately calibrated or assessed, might reflect or exacerbate biases from their training data. In healthcare contexts, these biases could lead to differential care for patients, potential misdiagnoses, or unwarranted tests and treatments, particularly for marginalized or underrepresented populations. With the growing integration of such models in healthcare, emphasis on fairness, transparency, and accountability becomes imperative to promote equitable care.
