About Me

I'm a Computer Science graduate student at New York University. My research revolves around the development and understanding of artificial intelligence, prioritizing its safety, reliability, and ethical implications. Below are two key directions that shape my exploration:

AI Ethics

Investigating the role of foundation models in the propagation and mitigation of Fake News and Misinformation: analyzing rumors, deepfakes, and devising prebunking strategies among other ethical considerations.

AI Alignment

Scalable Oversight: effectively monitor, manage, and guide the behavior of artificial intelligence systems, particularly as they increase in complexity and capability.

Earlier in my academic journey, I delved into computational sustainability during my undergrad and master's studies. I was captivated by the prospect of leveraging technology to provide solutions to the pressing societal and environmental challenges of the 21st century. Explore my publications on computational sustainability here.
