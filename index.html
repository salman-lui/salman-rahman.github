Salman Rahman

Computer Science, New York University

I'm a Computer Science Ph.D. student at New York University, dedicated to the development of safe, interpretable, and reliable artificial intelligence. Central to my work is addressing alignment problems in AI, striving to ensure that models resonate with shared human values. My research aims to unveil the mysteries within neural networks through mechanistic interpretability. The challenges in the healthcare domain drive my passion, prompting me to innovate solutions that are both precisely aligned and easily interpretable for medical applications. Dive deeper into my research here.

My earlier academic journey led me to explore computational sustainability during my undergrad and master's studies. I was captivated by the prospect of leveraging technology to provide solutions to the pressing societal and environmental challenges of the 21st century.
